{First name} {Last name} Apply for a pilot certificate

To conduct advanced operations, including flying drones up to 25kg in controlled airspace, near or over people, you will need a Pilot Certificate - Advanced Operations.

To conduct complex operations, including flying drones between 25kg to 150kg in controlled airspace, near or over people, or flying beyond visual line-of-sight (BVLOS) in uncontrolled airspace, you will need a Pilot Certificate - Level 1 Complex Operations

Before applying, you must have passed an Advanced Exam (and Complex (Level 1) Exam if applicable), and have successfully completed a flight review. After completing your flight review, the Flight Reviewer will submit your results electronically. You will be notified when you may proceed with this application.

There is a $25 non-refundable fee to apply for a Pilot certificate - Advanced Operations and a $125 non-refundable fee to apply for a Pilot Certificate - Level 1 Complex Operations.

Once issued, your pilot certificate does not expire. Keep your skills up-to-date by completing a training activity within the previous 24 months of operating a drone. You must keep proof on you that you’ve retained your knowledge whenever you fly.

Eligibility

For advanced operations:

You must:

  • be 16 years of age or older
  • have passed the Advanced Exam
  • have passed an advanced flight review performed by a drone flight reviewer to verify your flying abilities within the last 12 months

For complex operations:

You must:

  • be 18 years of age or older
  • have passed the Complex (Level 1) Exam
  • have passed a complex flight review performed by a drone flight reviewer to verify your flying abilities within the last 12 months
  • have a physician's endorsement that you meet RPAS medical standards
  • have proof of completion of ground school and flight training from a drone flight school, manufacturer or third-party organization

RPAS medical standards

General Conditions

The applicant shall be in a physical and mental condition to safely perform RPAS duties. This includes being able to:

  • Sustain attention, concentration, and alertness for the planned duration of RPAS duties;
  • Understand and effectively use the controls required for normal and emergency operations of the RPAS system that will be used;
  • Understand and follow all relevant instructions, including applicable requirements and prohibitions, for the specific RPAS system and airspace in which it will be used;
    • Safely and effectively communicate with anyone involved with the RPAS operation; and, Reliably identify objects within the flight geography of the aircraft, where required (this may be aided by technology, and visual observers where appropriate).

Cognitive impairment (including dementia)

The applicant’s cognitive functions necessary for piloting shall not be impaired.

Mental Health Illness or Disorders

  • The applicant shall not have previous or current intent to harm themselves or others.
  • The applicant shall not have a history or recurrent reckless, aggressive, or impulsive behavior.
  • The applicant shall not have a history of psychosis, psychotic episode, or mania.
  • Applicants with a previous or current mental health disorder (either suspected or confirmed) must meet the following criteria:
  • the condition must be in remission or controlled with minimal symptomatology;
  • the applicant must have sufficient insight to stop RPAS operations if their condition deteriorates; and,
  • the functional abilities necessary for RPAS operations must not be impaired.

Medications and other substances

Medications (prescribed, non-prescribed or natural health products)

The applicant’s functional abilities shall not be impaired by any medication during RPAS operations.

Alcohol or Substance Use Disorder, Substance Abuse, Substance Dependence, or Problematic Substance Use

Applicants with previous or current Alcohol or Substance Use Disorder, Substance Abuse, Substance Dependence, or Problematic Substance Use shall be deemed in remission by treating/monitoring physician; and the functional abilities necessary for RPAS operations shall not be impaired

Drugs (including Alcohol/Cannabis)

Applicants shall not be impaired by any drug or substance during RPAS operations.
