{First name} {Last name} Submit flight review results

The purpose of a flight review is to verify the flying abilities against Transport Canada standards. The flight review is a necessary step to obtaining a Pilot Certificate - Advanced or Level 1 Complex Operations. It can only be done after the pilot candidate has passed an Advanced Exam (for Advanced Operations) or a Complex (Level 1) Exam (for Complex Operations).

When presenting themselves for a flight review, the pilot candidate must provide you, the flight reviewer, with government-issued identification and a copy of their pilot certificate. Before proceeding with a flight review, you must verify that the pilot candidate's identification matches that of their pilot certificate.

For complex operations, you will also need to verify the pilot candidate's physical endorsement of RPAS medical standards and proof of completion of ground school and flight training.

During the flight review, you must verify each skill individually on the flight review checklist. You must then submit the results online to Transport Canada for the pilot candidate. If a flight review is unsuccessful, candidates must wait 24 hours before another flight review may be conducted.

Eligibility

The pilot candidate must:

  • be at least 16 years of age for an Advanced flight review, or 18 years of age for a Complex (Level 1) flight review
  • have a pilot certificate and passed either the Advanced Exam or Complex (Level 1) Exam
  • provide government-issued identification with the correct spelling of their name
  • for a Complex (Level 1) flight review:
    • provide a physician endorsement that the pilot meets RPAS medical standards or has obtained a Category 1 or 3 medical certificate
    • provide proof of completion of ground school and flight training from a drone flight school, manufacturer or third-party organization

What you need before you start

It will take 5 minutes to submit flight review results. To begin, you will need the following information from the pilot candidate:

  • a copy of their pilot certificate
  • a valid (not expired) piece of government-issued (federal/provincial/territorial/state government authority or the equivalent body abroad) identification that provides their name and date of birth
    • a driver's license that is issued by a province or territory or the equivalent abroad
    • a passport
    • a birth certificate or baptismal certificate, certified by the issuing authority, or a duly notarized copy. If the date of birth is not shown on a baptismal certificate, it must be supported by a statutory declaration in which the candidate declares the date of birth
    • a Certificate of Registration of Birth Abroad issued by the Department of Citizenship and Immigration
    • a citizenship certificate
    • a permanent resident card
    • an aviation personnel permit or license, showing the date of birth, issued by the state of which the candidate is a citizen
    • a military identification
    • a Certificate of Indian Status
    • an identification card issued by the federal, provincial or territorial government
  • the Certificate of Registration for the drone used for the flight review
  • for a Complex (Level 1) flight review:
    • a copy of a valid RPAS medical certificate or Category 1 or 3 medical certificate
    • proof of completion of ground school and flight training from a drone flight school, manufacturer or third-party organization
  • the flight review date and results
